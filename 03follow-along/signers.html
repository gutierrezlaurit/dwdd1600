Who were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include:

Thomas Jefferson
The primary author of the Declaration and later the third U.S. president.
John Adams
A strong advocate for independence, later the second U.S. president.
Benjamin Franklin
A diplomat, inventor, and elder statesman who helped secure French support for the revolution.
John Hancock
President of the Continental Congress, known for his large, bold signature.
Samuel Adams
A leader in the push for independence and a key figure in organizing resistance to British rule.
Richard Henry Lee
Introduced the resolution for independence in the Continental Congress.
Roger Sherman
The only person to sign all four major U.S. founding documents.
Charles Carroll
The only Catholic signer and one of the wealthiest men in the colonies.

These men, along with 48 others, risked their lives and fortunes by declaring independence from Britain in 1776.
