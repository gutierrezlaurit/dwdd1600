Alfajores de maizena

  • 200 gr of Salted Butter
  • 200 gr of flour
  • 300 gr of Cornstarch
  • 1/2 tsp of Baking Soda
  • 2 tsp of Bakind Powder
  • 150 gr of Powder sugar
  • 3 egg yolks
  • 1 tbsp of conac
  • 1 tsp of Vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp of lemon juice
  • Dulce de leche (for filling)

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, beat the butter with the sugar until smooth, add the vanilla essence, the lemon zest and beat for a few more seconds.
  2. Add the yolks one at a time, mixing everything well.
  3. Finally, add the dry ingredients by integrating them with a spatula and without kneading.
  4. Let cool for a few minutes, stretch ½ cm thick on a counter sprinkled with cornflour and cut the cookies.
  5. Place the lids on a plate and cook over moderate heat for 15 minutes without letting them color.
  6. Place the lids on a baking sheet and cook over moderate heat for 15 minutes until they turn a light yellow color.
  7. Assemble the alfajores with the dulce de leche and pass them through grated coconut.
What is an alfajor?
South American dessert that features two melt-in-your-mouth cookies with dulce de leche sandwiched in between them
What is dulce de leche?
Dulce de leche is a thick, rich caramel sauce made from one ingredient — sweetened condensed milk.
